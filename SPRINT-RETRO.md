## What was cut, and why

- **500+ pages → 267.** The gap was only reachable via unverified legal
  content or near-duplicate comparison pages. The charter said quality wins;
  the integrity rule ("never publish an unverified value") is the product's
  moat, so it governed. The engine now grows pages *automatically* as data is
  verified (hubs publish at ≥4 fields — Missouri is one field away).
- **State-comparison pages.** Deferred until per-state coverage deepens;
  currently they'd be two thin columns. Revisit at ~8 verified fields/state.
- **Rent-receipt state pages + remaining 38 termination states.** Converted
  into the §6 research recipe in OPERATIONS.md (30–45 min/field, weekly).
- **Journalist emails in the CRM.** Individual bylines can't be verified from
  here; inventing them poisons outreach. The CRM lists verified-active
  publications + how to find the current contact in 2 minutes.

## Highest-risk assumptions (watch these)

1. **Indexing/authority is the bottleneck, not content.** 267 solid pages
   mean little until referring domains exist. If impressions are flat at
   week 4, OPERATIONS §4 shifts everything to outreach — trust that rule.
2. **The hub threshold (≥4 fields) is enough substance.** If GSC shows hubs
   getting impressions but no clicks, they may need worked examples per state
   (the guides pattern) — that's content depth, not more pages.
3. **Medium-confidence rent-increase/deposit-return values.** They're
   displayed with flags, but they're the least-verified data on high-traffic
   pages. The staleness script will keep surfacing them; §6 them first.
4. **Solo-operator cadence.** The system assumes 4–8 real hours/week. If a
   month passes with no §1 loop, nothing breaks — but nothing compounds
   either. The loop is the product now.
