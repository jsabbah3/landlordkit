1. FACT: Only ~15 US states require landlords to pay interest on security deposits. The other 36 require exactly nothing. Full ranked list: [interest report]
2. MYTH: "It's in the lease, so it's enforceable." Not for late fees — 14 states cap them by statute, and a fee above the cap is generally void *even though the tenant signed*.

**W2**
4. FACT: Florida's month-to-month termination notice has been 30 days since July 2023 (HB 1417) — most "state law" tables online still say 15. This is why verification dates matter.
5. MYTH: "Deposit interest rates don't change if the law doesn't." CT, MD, DC, IL and ND publish a NEW rate every year — compliant last year ≠ compliant now.

**W3**
7. FACT: Deposit return deadlines range from 14 days (AK/AZ/HI) to 60 (AL/AR). Miss yours and many states award the tenant 2–3× the deposit + attorney fees.
8. MYTH: "Normal wear and tear" is deductible. It isn't, anywhere. Faded paint and worn carpet are the cost of doing business; damage beyond that is deductible — with itemization.
9. TOOL: Deadline + itemized statement generator for your state: [deposit-return tracker]

**W4**
10. FACT: Maine has the strictest late-fee rule in the country: 4% max, and you can't charge it until rent is 15 days late.
11. MYTH: "30 days notice is always enough to raise rent." CA needs 90 for >10% increases; NY scales 30/60/90 with tenancy length; and mid-lease you usually can't raise at all.
12. TOOL: Generate a compliant rent-increase notice (right period, PDF): [rent-increase generator]

**W5**
13. FACT: Massachusetts landlords owe 5% interest (or the bank rate) on deposits held a year — and must keep the deposit in a separate MA bank account. Most other states: no such rule.
14. MYTH: "I paid the deposit back, we're done." If your state requires interest, the return must include it — the deposit alone can leave you short.
