# HARO / Connectively / Qwoted playbook

**What:** journalists post source requests; you answer as "founder of
LandlordKit, maintainer of statute-cited datasets on US landlord-tenant law."
Each placement = a quote + usually a backlink from real press.

**Setup (Jake, once):** create free source accounts on Connectively (ex-HARO),
Qwoted, and Featured. Set alerts/keywords: landlord, rental, security deposit,
rent increase, eviction, tenant, property management, real estate investing.

**Weekly (15–20 min):** scan alerts; answer ONLY where our data genuinely
helps (2–3/week max). Speed wins — answer within 2–3 hours of the query when
possible.

**Rules:** real name, real credential, only claims our data supports, include
one specific number + the statute, offer the dataset/CSV as follow-up. Never
pay for links; never fabricate expertise (you're a data maintainer + landlord,
not a lawyer — say so).

## 5 pre-drafted templates

1. **"How much can a landlord charge in late fees?"** — 14 states cap fees by
statute, from Maine's 4% to NM/TN/VA at 10%; New York caps at the lesser of
$50 or 5% (RPL §238-a). A fee above the cap is generally unenforceable even
when the lease says otherwise. [credential line]

2. **"What should renters know about security deposits?"** — Two clocks matter:
the return deadline (14–60 days by state) and interest (required in ~15 states
— Massachusetts owes 5%/yr, and cities like Chicago set their own). Missing
the deadline costs the landlord 2–3× the deposit in many states. [credential]

3. **"Rent increase rules?"** — The notice period is state law, not lease
preference: 30 days is common, but California requires 90 for increases over
10% (Civ. Code §827) and New York scales 30/60/90 with tenancy length.
Statewide caps exist only in CA, OR, and WA. [credential]

4. **"Biggest legal mistakes small landlords make?"** — Three we see in the
data: late fees above the state cap (void, and they weaken evictions),
missing the deposit-return deadline (treble damages in CO/MD/MA), and missing
the Jan 31 1099-NEC deadline for contractors ($60–$310+ per form). [credential]

5. **"How are landlord-tenant laws changing?"** — Quietly and fast: Florida
moved month-to-month termination notice from 15 to 30 days (HB 1417, 2023);
California capped most deposits at one month (AB 12, 2024); five states
publish new deposit-interest rates every year. Most online tables lag these —
which is why we stamp every value with a last-verified date. [credential]
